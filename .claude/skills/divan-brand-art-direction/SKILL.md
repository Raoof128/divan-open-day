# DIVAN Brand and Art Direction

## Outcome

Create a museum-grade Persian poetry experience that feels illuminated, editorial, tactile, restrained, modern, and culturally specific. The work must look intentionally designed rather than generically AI-generated.

## Locked direction

- Illuminated Persian miniature brought to life.
- Twilight garden leading to a candlelit reading alcove.
- Lapis, pomegranate, manuscript gold, parchment, cypress, warm brass, and charcoal ink.
- English poem first, Persian directly beneath it.
- The poem remains the visual and semantic focal point.
- Ornament supports hierarchy and never competes with reading.

## Reject

- generic purple AI gradients;
- neon fantasy;
- excessive glassmorphism;
- fake Arabic or Persian letterforms;
- generated text inside visual assets;
- unlicensed manuscript scans;
- floating decorations over poem text;
- inconsistent radii, shadows, spacing, or perspective;
- visual spectacle that delays the first useful screen.

## Composition contract

1. Keep the book and candle within the mobile safe centre.
2. Reserve a text-safe region before approving generated assets.
3. Keep critical subjects visible in both dedicated 9:16 and 16:9 renders.
4. Never rely on cropping the desktop asset to produce the mobile composition.
5. Use one shared style preamble, palette, light direction, material language, and camera grammar across generated assets.

## Typography

- Use repository-bundled or system fonts only.
- Preserve real Persian text as live HTML with `lang="fa" dir="rtl"`.
- Avoid decorative display faces for long poem passages.
- Do not distort Persian shaping through letter spacing or text transforms.

## Review output

When reviewing a visual change, return:

- what supports the locked direction;
- what feels generic, culturally inaccurate, or noisy;
- exact component or asset corrections;
- mobile and reduced-motion implications.

Read `references/tokens.md` before changing visual tokens.
